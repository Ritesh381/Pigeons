Energy consumption at home is directly tied to carbon emissions because much of our energy comes from burning fossil fuels like coal, oil, and natural gas. These fuels release CO₂, a major contributor to climate change. For example, every time we use electricity from a coal -powered grid, cook with LPG, or drive fuel-powered vehicles, we add to our carbon footprint. Simpl e actions, like switching to energy- efficient appliances or renewable energy, can cut down emissions significantly, helping us protect the planet while saving on energy costs.

"Ironing your clothes for just 10 minutes emits around 200 grams of CO₂"

Impact of Household Appliances and Transportation:

  1. CO₂ from Cooking with LPG:
    • LPG emits approximately 2.9 kg of CO₂ per liter burned.
    • For a household using 10 liters of LPG per month, this translates to 10 liters times 2.9 kg = 29 kg of CO₂ per month
  2. Fuels for Transportation and Carbon Emissions
    • Petrol/Diesel Vehicles:
    • A car emitting 2.3 kg of CO₂ per liter of petrol consumed could quickly add several tons
      of CO₂ annually for a family.
